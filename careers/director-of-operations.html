Director of Operations

We are a small company, laser focused on creating complex application interfaces. As our client engagements have grown in size and complication, and as we look to expand our design team for the first time in our four year history, we are ready to hire a Director of Operations to help us chart our course.

You’ll have a broad charge and a lot of responsibilities, but you’ll be working with a kick-ass team that supports you. You will be challenged every single day and given the opportunity to make a real impact from the moment you join. You’ll have oversight over the following areas of the business, including account and project management, office management, marketing, communication, and HR.

Accounts/Projects:

During the sales process:

  • Work with leadership team to determine project details, scope, timeline, and price
  • Produce contract and manage contractual process through signing
  • Manage CRM platform throughout entire client relationship

During the project:

  • Work with leadership team to prepare production team for project execution
  • Work with leadership team to manage weekly resources, bandwidth, backlog
  • Keep track of and report on project health on a weekly basis
  • Manage project management tools (including production team’s daily involvement with tools)

Upon project completion:

  • Arrange post-project client interview
  • Arrange and work with leadership team to lead postmortem with project team
  • Arrange review between client and agency review site(s)

Office:

  • Manage vendor relationships - landlord, security, IT, etc.
  • Greet clients in person and on phone
  • Ensuring office presents well at all times
  • Manage kitchen/office supply inventory
  • Manage office daily opening and closing, and communication around office delays and closings

Marketing:

  • Oversee production of internal projects
  • Manage/ oversee production of Bravery
  • Oversee and promote Brave’s online reputation (SEO, press, etc.)
  • Maintain social and professional networks (Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn, Dribbble, etc.)

Communication:

  • Daily: Kick off huddle, highlighting each day’s high level moments
  • Weekly: Monitor and provide sprint status/health across all projects
  • Monthly: Write a “month in review” update for internal distribution
  • Quarterly: Report on holistic project performance
  • Annually: Report on holistic company performance
  • Establish and maintain a quarterly Brave update for our clients and partners

Personnel:

  • Manage onboarding program for employees
  • Manage Brave Guidebook and Employee Handbook
  • Organize weekly team lunches, monthly nights out, and quarterly overnight retreats
