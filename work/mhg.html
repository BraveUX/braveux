Sales Management Platform

We helped Miller-Heiman take its research-proven sales methodology out of the spreadsheet and into a living, tangible online tool, where individuals and teams alike can collaborate and win on sales opportunities.

Challenge

It's All In The Numbers

Sales and marketing teams are no stranger to spreadsheets, but a lack of consistency and a clear starting point of patterns is holding the format back. Insights take hours to derive, and different users might reach different conclusions.

Solution

Highlight Better Habits

We visualized the pipelines and opportunities for the user, so that we can guide them directly to what needs attention now. Standardized components make success repeatable, and historical patterns start to emerge - all in an intuitive format.

Scope

  • Information Architecture
  • Interaction Design

A true north for sales

Savvy salespeople often keep track of everything in their head. Our navigation has to be intuitive, while helping bear some of the cognitive load. We keep the user focused on the task at head, without losing their place in their overall to-do list.

A vision board

Rolodexes and spreadsheets are both great at cramming a lot of data into one place, but they don’t capture the why. Our system of ranked, curated cards empower users to quickly triage through what matters today.

Smooth Transitions

Numbers alone only communicate so much. We use a system of themed color, in addition to labels and iconography, to help instant recognition of what's performing well, and what needs more attention.

Flow through a funnel

Is your funnel in shape? It’s not enough to just show a list of opportunities. Our visualizations help users quickly understand what’s expiring, what’s ahead, and most importantly, what’s at risk.

Thinking in blue
and green

Curation can only get us so far. Sometimes we need to show our work. Our evolution of Miller-Heiman’s Blue and Green sheets pack a punch, in a much more attractive package. Plus, our co-branding with Miller-Heiman’s system of products means that users always know what they're looking at.

A face to name

How do you create a symbol for a gut feeling? We worked with Miller-Heiman to help visualize the various ebbs and flows of the sales process, so that users are less a series of numbers in a database, and more of a strategic position in opportunities to be won.
