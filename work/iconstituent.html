eGovernment Communications Platform

We helped iConstituent take the same modern UX that apps and the rest of the internet enjoys to the halls of government, so that elected officials can spend less time with paperwork, and more time representing the citizens they service.

Challenge

Official Use Only

Elected officials in localities big and small constantly juggle polarizing topics, strict guidelines, and a passionate user base. How can they make sure each voice is heard while adhering to the standards of law?

Solution

People Over Process

We humanized the constituent and brought a tangible feeling to procedure and paperwork, resulting in an interface that is not only easy to learn, but ensures government offices never miss a beat.

Scope

  • Information Architecture
  • Interaction Design

United voices

Big issues bring hundreds of voices together. We created layouts that help users identify patterns and common themes in incoming messages, while preserving the ability to zero in on each unique message.

Signed, sealed, delivered

Every constituent deserves a reply. Our letter flows empower each representative office to put together the best answers in a collaborative, fluid environment. Flexible, modular content pieces can be quickly assembled, all under official letterhead.

A quick show of hands

Even the smallest districts process thousands of communications a year. Batch views let users rapidly review outgoing messages for accuracy, so that no letter is ever a waste of time.

A key to the city

Navigating a deep stack of requests, cases, and communications is error-prone and time-consuming. Our new navigation structure highlights what users need to do next, while keeping a smarter set of 'just in case' features on every screen.

Snap polls

Interruptions are inevitable. Our global actions let a user respond quickly to an incoming call, without losing their place, reducing errors and increasing user happiness.

A positive ID

Each constituent and community group is more than just one issue. We created views that are both comprehensive and consistent, so that each user can see the broader context that drives voters in their community.

We had big dreams of connecting people and government, and an aggressive timeline, but no clear next steps. Brave quickly jumped in and guided us through an intuitive and organized design process.

The collaboration was intense, but we enjoyed being challenged by some of the brightest resources we’ve ever worked with: to question assumptions, consider unique solutions, and never forget what’s best for our users.

We walked away with a superb new design, but more importantly, we are a changed team, with a renewed commitment to our mission, our clients, and our users.

Michael Cohen, Vice President, Sales and Product Strategy, iConstituent
