User-Centric Safety Mobile App

We designed a testable prototype to promote LiveSafe’s long-term business goals and create a better in-app experience for users.

Challenge

Distinguishing LiveSafe from a user’s organization

Who is LiveSafe? Users are confused by who owns each feature in the app and LiveSafe gets lost in the mix.

Solution

Clarifying who’s the boss

The new prototype clearly differentiates LiveSafe from the organizations using the app.

Scope

  • Information Architecture
  • Mobile App Design
  • Prototype Testing

A proper 
starting point

The new homepage and bottom navigation provide the user with everything they need in one, easy to find place. The newsfeed shows recent updates while the map orients the user, providing a natural entry point into SafeWalk.

Don’t be a bystander

LiveSafe’s app empowers users to anonymously or publicly report tips to their organization’s security team. Users’ can attach photos or videos, then message security with more details to do their part in keeping themselves and others safe.

Know where you are

We took a complicated map and simplified it, providing the user with additional information and better safety features. Campus buildings now have a context panel that links to related resources and users’ have an additional entry point into SafeWalk.

Proven by research

We created an interactive mobile prototype and sat down with actual users, to explore their safety concerns, habits, and mental models. This data validated our approach, and identified refinements that we integrated into LiveSafe’s product launch.

Resources when and where you need them

Resources are great to have, but only if the user can find them. We gave a resources tab to keep them all organized and accessible.
